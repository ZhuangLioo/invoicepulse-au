# Business Case

## Problem

Australian small businesses often manage invoices through Xero, MYOB, QuickBooks, Excel, or a mix of admin workflows. These tools can create invoices, but users still need to manually inspect exported lists to decide who to follow up.

The pain is operational:

- overdue invoices are not always prioritised clearly
- missing due dates and contact emails slow down follow-up
- repeat late-paying customers are hard to spot from a flat CSV
- small teams spend time writing payment reminders manually
- invoice data quality problems can hide inside accounting exports

## Proposed Solution

InvoicePulse AU provides a browser-based invoice health check. The user uploads a CSV, confirms column mapping, and receives a dashboard, risk-ranked follow-up list, customer risk summary, data quality warnings, reminder drafts, and exportable reports.

## Target Users

- Australian small business owners
- consultants and service businesses
- tradies and subcontractors
- small agencies
- bookkeepers and admin staff
- portfolio reviewers assessing BA/System Analyst capability

## Value Proposition

InvoicePulse AU turns invoice data into action:

- who owes money
- how much is overdue
- which invoices are highest priority
- which customers are risky
- what data quality issues need fixing
- what reminder can be sent today

## Why Not Build A Full Accounting Tool

Xero and MYOB already cover invoice creation, payment recording, and accounting workflows. InvoicePulse AU deliberately avoids replacing those products. It sits beside them as a lightweight CSV-based diagnostic layer.

## Success Criteria

- A first-time user understands the product in under 30 seconds.
- A user can upload sample data and run an analysis without setup.
- The tool correctly excludes draft, void, and cancelled invoices from receivables.
- The top follow-up list gives a clear next action.
- The project demonstrates BA and Systems Analyst thinking through code and documentation.
